SQL Task 3: Simple Aggregation and Grouping

1. Count Total Employees

Command:
SELECT COUNT(*) AS TotalEmployees
FROM Employees;

Explanation:
Counts total number of rows in the Employees table.

---

2. Average Salary

Command:
SELECT AVG(Salary) AS AverageSalary
FROM Employees;

Explanation:
Calculates average salary of all employees.

---

3. Total Salary

Command:
SELECT SUM(Salary) AS TotalSalary
FROM Employees;

Explanation:
Calculates total salary of all employees.

---

4. Count Employees per Department

Command:
SELECT Department, COUNT(*) AS EmployeeCount
FROM Employees
GROUP BY Department;

Explanation:
Groups employees by department and counts how many are in each.

---

5. Average Salary per Department

Command:
SELECT Department, AVG(Salary) AS AvgSalary
FROM Employees
GROUP BY Department;

Explanation:
Calculates average salary for each department.

---

6. Filter Groups using HAVING

Command:
SELECT Department, COUNT(*) AS EmployeeCount
FROM Employees
GROUP BY Department
HAVING COUNT(*) > 1;

Explanation:
Returns only departments with more than 1 employee.

---

7. Combine WHERE + GROUP BY + HAVING

Command:
SELECT Department, AVG(Salary) AS AvgSalary
FROM Employees
WHERE Age > 22
GROUP BY Department
HAVING AVG(Salary) > 50000;

Explanation:

* WHERE filters rows first
* GROUP BY groups data
* HAVING filters grouped results
